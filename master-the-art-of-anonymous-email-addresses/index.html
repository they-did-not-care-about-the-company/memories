Master The Art Of Anonymous Email Addresses

If you’re using email, you already know it’s a very convenient and easy way to communicate. Just write a message, click “Send” and the other side will get it almost immediately. 

Even if they are on another continent.

Few online communication channels can do that, at least not for free or as fast as email.

But email has its downsides. One of the biggest, without a doubt, is how much it can reveal about you when you don’t want it.

If you’re a regular Gmail user and don’t care much about privacy, only convenience, then this article might not be interesting for you.

However, if for whatever reason, you need to be anonymous when sending and receiving emails, then keep reading. 

You might get some good anonymous email address ideas.

Why Do You Need An Anonymous Email Account?

You might need an anonymous email address for a lot of reasons.

It could be because you want to prank a friend with a fake email address. 

Or, more seriously, you might need to send an anonymous email to social services.

To report on corruption or fraud in your company.

To warn people about something your government doesn’t want them to know.

And the list goes on.

Whatever the reason(s), knowing how to create an anonymous email account is a good idea. 

So let’s talk about how to do it.

How to Create An Anonymous Email Account Free?

First, yes it is possible to set up an anonymous email account with Gmail, Microsoft Outlook or Yahoo. 

Problem is, it takes a lot of work and it’s 100% bullet-proof. 

Let me explain.

For instance, it is possible to create an account on Gmail using a fake username. 

Problem is, Google will ask you for a phone number. This is because it wants to send an SMS message and verify you’re human and not a bot.

There are two ways around this:

  • Use a burner phone.
  • Use an online SMS service like Receive SMS.

Problem is, even with these precaution measures, you still aren’t fully anonymous. 

Gmail and your ISP will know where and who you are thanks to your IP address.

So now, you need a way to hide your IP address somehow.

One way to do it is to sign up for a VPN service. 

How does a VPN help?

In short, a Virtual Private Network software will encrypt your data before it reaches the ISP or Google. That way, the data first goes through the VPN and from there to its destination, like a website

That website will see the traffic as if it is coming from the VPN server and not your location.

In other words, they will see the VPN’s IP and not yours.

However, you still aren’t out of hot water.

For one, the VPN might be keeping logs or payment records. If so, these can include identifying information.

Besides, you also need to consider under whose jurisdiction the VPN is established. They might still be obligated to provide information if the government asks for it.

So, there are a lot of “ifs” here.
